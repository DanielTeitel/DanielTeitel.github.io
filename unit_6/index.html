Play in April

The Game

Play in April exists to recognise the importance of play for human growth and development. It is one month of the year that takes play very seriously, sort of, by encouraging playfulness across Australia – at home, in the community, at school and at work.


Play in April is here to remind us to play, and to help us play more in everyday life. It is also here to help raise funds for The Global Play Alliance, a group of organisations that help build playgrounds in developing countries with local communities. Headed by Playground Ideas, the Global Play Alliance provides stimulating playgrounds for young children who would otherwise not be able to access the kind of playground that supports important mental, physical and creative development.

How to Play

There are so many ways to play. The National Day of Play marks the end of Play Month – a month of games and activities designed to help you bring play into your life. Simply select whether you’d like to play as an individual, group, business or school.

Individual

Bring play into your life. Find ideas, games and activities that will open you up to the power of play.

Group

Play with friends. Find tools to arrange play events for you and your friends or family. Play in community and raise money to support play all over the world.

Business

Bring play into your workplace. Find games and activities that build creativity, develop leadership skills, help solve problems and imagine new solutions.

Schools

Work together as a school to make learning more fun and to help giver other children around the world the chance to grow up healthy, happy and with a future.
